You may be able to get help with the cost of visiting a close relative or partner in prison if you’re aged over 18, are on certain benefits or have a low income.

You might not need to answer all the questions, depending on your circumstances.

Start now

Before you start

You’ll need to have:

  • Your National Insurance number
  • Prison number of the prison where the prisoner related to you is held
  • A valid award notice (if claiming Universal credit, child tax credit, pension credit or health certificate)
  • Proof of parentage - eg Long birth certificate (if acting as an escort for the prisoner's child)
  • Letter from the doctor (if acting as a medical escort for another adult who qualifies for assistance)

Other ways to apply

You can apply by filling in a paper form instead.

You will need to complete the paper form and send it to the address on the form to get help with travel costs.
